The practice was formed by Architects Kristian + Kay Hyde in 2006. The heart of the practice is a close collaboration between the following people...

Kristian Alexander Hyde

Director/Architect

BA[Hons] DIP[Arch] RIBA

Director of Hyde + Hyde Architects. Unit leader at the Welsh School of Architecture, visiting critic at the University of Portsmouth and University of Bath.


He graduated with a first class honours in Architecture from the University of Portsmouth in 2000. He then travelled to the USA and Canada studying Art, Philosophy and Architecture at the University of Cincinnati and went on to work for Anshen + Allen in San Francisco.


His work has won numerous awards and has been published in various broadsheets, academic journals and has been exhibited at the RIBA in London.

Kay Hyde

Director/Architect

BA[Hons] DIP[Arch] RIBA

Graduated with a first class honours degree in Architecture from the University of Portsmouth and University of Plymouth.


After graduating she joined Hampshire County Council, where she worked as a project architect on numerous schools and educational buildings. She then travelled to the United States to Study Architecture & Dance in Cincinnati and work in San Francisco to return in 2006 to work in London and later for John Pardey Architects in Lymington.


In 2006 Kay set up Hyde + Hyde Architects with her husband Kristian; a progressive award winning architecture practice based in South Wales, with work across the UK.

Claire de Jong

Project Coordinator

Claire's career began as sales ledger assistant for ECS International, a French computer company back in 1990.


In 1996, having gained experience in varying roles including credit control, recruitment and merchandising, Claire enjoyed five years working for global record label Decca Music Group in Finance & Business as Executive assistant to the Vice President.


After a career break to raise a family in 2004 Claire became a director with her partner within the automotive industry. With the sale of the company in 2010, she joined Hyde + Hyde Architects to pursue a long term passion for architecture.

David John

Office Manager

David John joined us in 2007 and brings with him 35 years of accountancy and a wealth of financial and project management experience.


Born in South Wales in 1950. After a commercial apprenticeship with Atlas Sprinkler studying ONC/HNC at Swansea College, he joined British Leyland and carried out positions covering financial costing, Investment Analysis, Business Planning and Forecasting.


At 40yrs of age John joined Alcoa Manufacturing as management Accountant in South Wales, covering all their Capital Investment of £4m, Cost Control of £17m and Financial Planning.


John took a sabbatical last year to fulfil a lifetime dream of sailing across the Atlantic.

James Kenyon

Architect

BA[Hons] MArch PGCert RIBA

James graduated from The University of Sheffield in 2009, receiving a commendation for his final year thesis project and the Sir HK Stephenson Travel Scholarship for MArch dissertation.


He has worked for award winning practices in the UK and internationally, including 12 months working on post Tsunami reconstruction projects in Sri Lanka. Most recently he played a pivotal role in the establishment of a new design led practice in Oxford.


His interest lies in research-based design, which is conceptually driven and responsive to context beyond just the aesthetic. His work has been published in the Architects Journal and exhibited at the RIAI in Dublin.

Stephanie Wilson

Brand Coordinator and Researcher

BA [Hons]

Stephanie graduated from the University of Exeter in 2015, attaining a BA in English Literature. During her undergraduate study, Stephanie directed her focus towards exploring the relationship between poetry, landscape and space through philosophical enquiry.


Prior to joining Hyde + Hyde in 2017, Stephanie completed an Art & Design Foundation, for which she was awarded a Distinction for her final exhibition piece responding to the Rietveld Schröder House. She was then appointed the University’s Artist in Residence which enabled her to exhibit her research enquiries.

v

Stephanie brings a wealth of literary knowledge to the practice and continues to develop her field as Hyde + Hyde’s Brand Coordinator and in-house Researcher.

Alister Webb

Part 1 Architectural Assistant

BSc [Hons]

Alister graduated from the Welsh School of Architecture, where he developed an interest for self-build designs, through collaborative work on a pavilion for a local coffee shop. Prior to obtaining his BSc from the WSA, Alister travelled to Zambia to work on a hospital construction project with Orkidstudio.


Alister’s interests are founded in architecture’s relationship with the phenomena of the natural world. His final year project encompassed the creation of a subterranean transport interchange, designed to both emulate and enhance the elemental lithology of the coasts of the South West.


When he’s out of the studio, Alister can usually be seen with tools or a longboard, evolving his fascinations of craft and nature.

Tayseer Kardash

Part 2 Architectural Assistant

BSc [Hons] MArch

Prior to joining Hyde + Hyde in the Spring of 2018, Tayseer worked for PAD Studio, an architectural firm specialising in contemporary sustainable design.

Originating from Sudan, Tayseer moved to the UK in 2009 in pursuit of furthering her architectural aspirations. Despite almost a complete loss of hearing, Tayseer exceeded all expectations.


In 2013, Tayseer graduated from the University of Portsmouth having received numerous awards including the Chappel Bequest and Dibben Prize, commending her outstanding architectural achievements. Tayseer proceeded to obtain her Masters from the Welsh School of Architecture in 2016 and has continued to receive considerable recognition. To date, Tayseer sits on RIBA’s Architects for Change panel and has been featured by RIBA as part of their Black History programme.
